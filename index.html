The Guilford County Budget

Welcome

Few people have the time to read the official 400-page budget document released by the Guilford County Government. On top of that, it is filled with spreadsheets and financial language that can be difficult to understand. While this may be necessary for Guilford officials to manage the millions of dollars in county assets, it is probably not the best format for tax-payers to understand the budget.

That’s where this guide comes in to play. Without the endless spreadsheets or complicated financial jargon, we'll break down some of the key areas Guilford County's budget that are of interest to Guilford residents. We'll also look at some of the ways Guilford services impact people's every-day lives. Hopefully, this interactive guide will help bring the budget "to life", making it more transparent and inclusive in the process.

Revenue Sources

The first important question to answer to better understand the budget is: where does the money come from? After all, without revenue, the budget would not exist.

As the pie chart below shows, the property tax is the largest contributor to Guilford County’s budget. At 63.7 %, the property tax makes up more than a majority of the revenue in Guilford County. The other largest contributors are the sales tax (14.8 %) and funds from Federal and State Governments (12.2 %). When combined, these three sources account for over 90% percent of county revenue. How Taxes Are Calculated

Property Tax

How much you pay in property tax in Guilford County is determined by the value of the property you own. Essentially it is a percentage of the total appraised value of your property. At less than one percent of total property value, this percentage is % 0.7305. Check out the graphic below for an example.

Sales Tax

The local sales tax in Guilford County is 2% of all local purchases. It can be determined by multiplying a purchase by 0.02. For example, a $1.00 purchase on a soft drink at a convenience store would garner a local sales tax of 2 cents.

Service Areas

Now that we know where Guilford County's budget revenue, the next question is: what services does the county provide? Because there are so many, grouping the numerous departments and programs into categories known as Service Areas helps to better navigate the budget by the type of service provided.

Guilford County services are divided into six distinct service groups: Education, Public Safety, Human Services, General Government, Support Services, and Community Services. This section gives a brief introduction these service groups and some of their programs. This will be helpful for when we look at the spending data in the next section.

Education

The Education service area aims to give Guilford County residents the knowledge and tools to be successful in a rapidly changing modern economy. While education funding is mostly a function of the State, all counties are expected to contribute. Like most counties, Guilford County's contribution exceeds the minimum required by law.

Public Safety works hard to ensure the safety of everyone in Guilford County. The services provided by public safety are far-reaching in scope. This includes things like emergency transportation in times of crisis, law enforcement, and animal services.

Public Safety

Human Services

Human Services programs are designed to help prevent and control disease, protect the well-being of children, and promote healthy lifestyles. They also help to ensure residents who are unable to provide for themselves and their families have access to temporary social services. Many of these programs are federally mandated.

Simply, General Government manages all the departments in the county. This includes planning, keeping records, making the budget, and legal direction. Also, departments in this area provide some services such as property assessment, property-tax collection, and conducting elections.

General Government

Support Services

Support Services provides administrative and operational support services for all other public services and infrastructure. This includes the maintenance of county facilities and information technology systems.

Community Services promotes community growth, economic development and job creation. Programs in this service area also provide outlets for recreation, life-skills education, and environmental protection.

Community Services

Conclusion

Hopefully, this guide has been helpful in making the Guilford County budget more inclusive and transparent. While we did not cover the budget in its entirety, we hit on some of the major concepts of it including:

  • Revenue Sources
  • Service Areas And Programs
  • The Allocation of Local Money
  • How Guilford Services Impact Residents' Everyday Lives

If you would like more information, Guilford County makes all of its budget information available to the public. After reading this guide, you should be more equipped to understand the terminology used, allowing you to look deeper into how locally-based revenue is allocated.

A better understanding of these topics can help you better know how to use Guilford Services when you or a loved one may need them. It can also help you be more informed on topics more civic in nature, such as deciding which policies to support or which candidates to vote for in local elections. With more a more informed public, we can work together to make the best use of our local funding so that Guilford County can continue to work to better allocate funds, guide policies, and improve services for Guilford Residents.
